Notes & Methodology

I created this data visualization to help raise awareness about our foodprint and how our daily choices can make a huge difference. We often underestimate the cummulative effect of small daily choices.

To create the visualization, I used the work "Environmental impacts of food production" by Hannah Ritchie and Max Roser1. This awsome collection of data allowed me to create meals by combining available products.

Althought it would have been interesting to add more ingredients that many people use on a daily basis (yogurt and butter for example), I made the choice to limit the scope of this work to this single source since data from different sources was very complex to compare, due to the different methods used to calculate the impact of each ingredient.

I also limited the visualization to the main ingredients of each recipe and had to perform a few approximation, for example using the data on tofu for edamame and adjusting the calculation to the amount required for a portion of each.

Bottomline, the numbers generated by this visualization are here to give a global overview on how our food choices impact our environment, and not to provide very precise numbers.
